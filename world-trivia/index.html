Amazing World Facts

The Great Wall of China

Contrary to popular belief, the Great Wall of China is not visible from space with the naked eye. This myth has been debunked by astronauts who have actually been to space!

Antarctica's Unique Status

Antarctica is the only continent without a time zone. Scientists there typically use the time zone of their home country or the nearest research station.

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon rainforest produces about 20% of the world's oxygen and is home to 10% of all known species on Earth. It's larger than the next two largest rainforests combined!

The Sahara Desert

The Sahara Desert is almost as large as the entire United States! It's also expanding southward at a rate of 30 miles per year due to climate change.

Northern Lights

The Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis) are caused by solar particles colliding with Earth's magnetic field. They can appear in many colors, but green is the most common.

Mariana Trench

The deepest part of Earth's oceans is the Mariana Trench in the Pacific, reaching depths of over 36,000 feet. If Mount Everest were placed in it, the peak would still be over a mile underwater!
