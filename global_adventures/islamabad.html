About Islamabad

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan, known for its well-organized structure, lush greenery, and beautiful Margalla Hills.

Top Attractions

Faisal Mosque

An iconic mosque, one of the largest in the world, known for its contemporary design.

Pakistan Monument

A national monument representing the four provinces and three territories of Pakistan.

Daman-e-Koh

A viewpoint in the Margalla Hills offering panoramic views of Islamabad.

Rawal Lake

An artificial reservoir providing water and a popular spot for picnics and boating.

Margalla Hills

The foothills of the Himalayas, offering numerous hiking trails and rich wildlife.

Culinary Delights of Islamabad

Sajji

Whole chicken or lamb, marinated and roasted over coals, a Balochi specialty popular in Islamabad.

Chicken Karahi

A spicy and savory chicken dish cooked in a traditional wok-like pan called a karahi.

Seekh Kebabs

Minced meat, usually lamb or beef, seasoned with spices and grilled on skewers.

My Islamabad Journey

Visiting Islamabad was a truly serene experience. The city's layout, with its wide, tree-lined streets and backdrop of the Margalla Hills, offers a sense of peace rarely found in a capital. I remember the crisp morning air during a hike in the hills, the breathtaking view from Daman-e-Koh, and the profound tranquility within the Faisal Mosque.

The blend of modernity and nature is seamless. Evenings were often spent exploring local markets, savoring the delicious street food, especially the aromatic biryani and freshly grilled kebabs. The warmth of the people and their hospitality made the trip even more memorable. Islamabad is not just a city; it's an experience that calms the soul and enriches the mind.
